A finite set. See TransfiniteSet. The straight listing implementations of the set concept must be finite, since memory, although large, cannot be infinite. Sets are "by lists" implementations of the concept (hence must be finite). Transfinite sets are "Boolean test" implementations, and hence can be infinite, but cannot be modified one element at a time to any great degree of success. <br/><br/>
